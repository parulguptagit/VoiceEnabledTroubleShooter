## Overview
When Bluetooth devices won't connect or keep disconnecting, try forgetting the device, resetting network settings, and reducing interference.

## Symptoms
- AirPods or other device won't pair
- Connection drops during use
- Device doesn't appear in Bluetooth list

## Step-by-Step Fix

### Step 1: Forget the Device
Go to Settings > Bluetooth. Find the device and tap the (i) on the right. Tap "Forget This Device." Then put the device back in pairing mode and select it again from the list. Voice: "In Settings, tap Bluetooth, then tap the i next to the device name and choose Forget This Device."

### Step 2: Reset Network Settings
Settings > General > Transfer or Reset iPhone > Reset > Reset Network Settings. This also resets Wi-Fi and VPN. You'll need to re-pair Bluetooth devices and rejoin Wi-Fi. Voice: "Go to Settings, General, then Transfer or Reset iPhone, Reset, and tap Reset Network Settings."

### Step 3: Reduce Interference
Turn off other Bluetooth devices nearby. Restart the iPhone and the accessory. Ensure the accessory is charged.

## When to Contact Apple Support
If the device never appears or multiple accessories fail, contact Apple Support.
